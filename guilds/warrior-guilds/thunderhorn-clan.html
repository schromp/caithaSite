The Thunderhorn Clan, a formidable faction of nomadic monster hunters, has become an integral part of daily life in Caitha. Originally established to protect the lands and people of the Kanee in the far north from monstrous threats, the Thunderhorn Clan's purpose and role have evolved over time.

Purpose and Role

The Thunderhorn Clan's primary purpose is to hunt down and eliminate dangerous monsters, ensuring the safety of the Kanee and the regions they inhabit. While their initial focus was on protection, changing leadership and a shifting world led to the faction to prioritize profits over the once religious motive. They now undertake monster-hunting contracts with bounties, offering their services as skilled fighters and protectors.

Backstory and Origins

The Thunderhorn Clan traces its roots back to the early days of Kanee civilization. The Kanee, inhabitants of the northern regions of Caitha, hold fire in high regard, worshipping it as the source of warmth, light, and civilization itself. They refer to fire as "roor" and embrace it fervently, following a unique religious belief that prohibits extinguishing fire under any circumstances. This fiery reverence also extended to the protection of the Kanee from wild beasts and monsters.
Founded with a religious intent, the Thunderhorn Clan embodied the Kanee's deep connection with fire. However, as centuries passed, the faction gradually distanced itself from the religious practices of their people. While traces of their religious origins can still be found in their guidelines and rules, the Thunderhorn Clan shifted its focus away from spirituality, opting for a more secular and profit-oriented path.

Symbolism

The Thunderhorn Clan's symbolism revolves around the horns, a distinctive feature of the Kanee. The Kanee have a tradition of painting their horns as a form of self-expression and ancestral honor. The Thunderhorn Clan incorporates this tradition into its symbolism, using painted horns to signify allegiance to the clan. Members may paint their horns with symbols representing their kills, such as specific types of monsters or the number of creatures defeated.
These painted horns serve as a reminder of their roots and their accomplishments.

Membership and Recruitment

To join the Thunderhorn Clan, individuals must be Kanee and embrace the nomadic lifestyle that the faction upholds. Prospective members undergo rigorous tests of strength and skill, proving their combat abilities and survival prowess. Upon successfully slaying their first monster, they undergo an initiation ritual that solidifies their place within the Thunderhorn Clan.

Leadership Structure

The Thunderhorn Clan operates with a relatively flat hierarchy, allowing for decentralized decision-making within each nomadic caravan. Each caravan has a leader at the front. They take charge of navigation, ensuring the welfare of the animals pulling the carts, and share logistics responsibilities with the quartermaster. Additionally, they oversee a small team of scouts and trackers who explore territories ahead, identifying potential threats, challenges, and suitable campsites.

At the apex of the leadership structure is the Eldest, who serves as the guiding force of the Thunderhorn Clan. This individual, typically the most experienced or longest-serving member, makes significant decisions, resolves disputes, and holds responsibility for the clan as a whole.

The quartermaster, responsible for logistics, cart maintenance, and the treasury, plays a crucial role in decision-making regarding communal purchases and the allocation of the clan's finances.

Skills and Training

Members of the Thunderhorn Clan are skilled fighters, honing their combat abilities and survival skills through rigorous training. While some possess the ability to wield primal fire magic, their level of proficiency is referred to as "primal" within the classification system of Caithan mages.
They see fire as a spiritual force that aids them in combat and protection. Training extends beyond combat, as members are also adept at caring for and handling the animals that accompany their caravans. One intriguing aspect of this is their use of blue fire in their magic. This deviation from the traditional red flames associated with their deity, "roor," became a source of conflict with religious Kanee groups. The clan's blue fire was seen as a deviation from the established norm, resulting in a fracture between the Thunderhorn Clan and the religious factions of the Kanee. This deviation led the Thunderhorn Clan to gradually distance themselves from the religious practices and spiritual connection to fire.

Lifestyle and Traditions

The Thunderhorn Clan adheres to a nomadic lifestyle, traversing the lands of Caitha in search of monster-hunting contracts and opportunities for profit. While the Kanee have settled in permanent locations, the Thunderhorn Clan maintains a traditionalistic approach, retaining the nomadic way of life as a reminder of their origins and resilience.

Certain traditions shape the clan's identity:

Upon arriving at a new location, the Thunderhorn Clan announces its presence with the thunderous sound of their horns, a distinctive call that signifies their arrival. Festivities and celebrations take place when two Thunderhorn Clan caravans cross paths on the road. The two groups unite, forming a larger camp where they engage in merriment, trade, and even the occasional exchange of members who wish to switch caravans.

For members of the Thunderhorn Clan, horn-painting takes on a different significance. It becomes a means of signifying their allegiance to the clan and showcasing their achievements and prowess in battle. Each member of the Thunderhorn Clan paints unique symbols, patterns, or marks on their horns. These symbols may represent the types of monsters they have slain or the number of kills they have achieved. For example, a Thunderhorn Clan member who has successfully defeated a frostwyrm might bear a symbol denoting their accomplishment. The painted horns serve as a visual record of their triumphs and experiences, acting as a constant reminder of their roots and the battles they have fought.

Within the Thunderhorn Clan, a sense of kinship and loyalty permeates every aspect of their lifestyle. While the focus may have shifted to profit and monster hunting, the Thunderhorn Clan remains a tight-knit community, supporting one another in times of peril and celebration.

Elders, experienced members who have dedicated their lives to the clan, hold positions of respect and authority. Their wisdom and guidance shape the decision-making process and help resolve disputes. The overall authority, known as the Exemplar, leads the Thunderhorn Clan, making important decisions that affect the clan as a whole.

Despite the shift away from religious practices, the Thunderhorn Clan retains a strong connection to their heritage. They carry with them the values of honor, bravery, and resilience that were once integral to their religious beliefs. Though their pursuits may be driven by profit and survival, the Thunderhorn Clan members often find solace and strength in the shared traditions and customs that unite them.

Relationship and Reputation

The Thunderhorn Clan holds a significant reputation in the world of Caitha. While their profit-oriented nature might raise some skepticism, they are generally respected for their formidable combat skills and their ability to neutralize monstrous threats. However, their deviation from religious practices and blue fire magic creates tensions with religious Kanee groups who view them as deviants.

The Thunderhorn Clan also attracts curiosity from mage guilds due to their utilization of fire magic. The mage guilds perceive the Thunderhorn Clan's magic as primitive, yet their blue flames intrigue them, sparking an interest in understanding the nature and origins of this unique manifestation of fire.
